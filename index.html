Pressurized Water Reactor

Converting nuclear fission into reliable electrical power through controlled neutron physics and thermodynamic cycles.

Reactor Core & Neutron Physics

Uranium-235 undergoes neutron-induced fission releasing ~200 MeV per reaction. The reactor operates at criticality (k-effective = 1). Water moderates neutrons while control rods regulate flux to maintain stable output.

Primary Coolant System

High-pressure water (~150 atm) circulates at temperatures exceeding 300Â°C. The pressurizer stabilizes pressure while pumps maintain forced circulation.

Steam Generator

A shell-and-tube heat exchanger isolates radioactive primary coolant. Secondary water undergoes phase change to drive the Rankine cycle.

Turbine & Generator

Steam expansion rotates turbine blades. Mechanical energy converts into electrical energy via electromagnetic induction.

Condenser

Exhaust steam condenses under vacuum conditions, increasing thermodynamic efficiency and enabling closed-loop operation.

Cooling Tower

A third cooling loop dissipates waste heat via evaporative cooling. The visible plume consists of condensed water vapor.
