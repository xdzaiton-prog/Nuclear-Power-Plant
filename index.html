Pressurized Water Reactor

A high-efficiency nuclear system converting controlled fission into electrical power through the Rankine thermodynamic cycle, neutron moderation, and multi-layered safety engineering.

Reactor Core & Neutron Physics

Uranium-235 undergoes neutron-induced fission, releasing approximately 200 MeV of energy per reaction. The reactor operates at criticality (k-effective = 1), meaning neutron production equals neutron losses. Water acts as a moderator, slowing neutrons to thermal energies and increasing fission probability. Control rods absorb excess neutrons to maintain stable reactor power levels.
Mass-to-energy conversion during fission follows Einstein’s equation E = mc², allowing immense energy release from small mass differences.

Primary Coolant System

The primary loop circulates pressurized water at approximately 150 atmospheres and temperatures above 300°C. This prevents boiling while enabling efficient heat removal. Reactor coolant pumps maintain circulation, and the pressurizer stabilizes pressure variations. The entire loop is radioactive and fully contained within engineered barriers.

Steam Generator & Heat Transfer

The steam generator is a shell-and-tube heat exchanger. Primary coolant transfers thermal energy through alloy tubes to the secondary loop. The two water systems never mix, ensuring radiological safety. Secondary water undergoes phase transition, producing high-pressure steam.
This supports the Rankine Cycle: water is vaporized, expanded through a turbine, condensed, and recirculated to continuously generate mechanical work.

Steam Turbine & Electrical Generation

High-pressure steam expands through turbine stages, converting thermal energy into rotational mechanical energy. The turbine shaft drives a synchronous generator, producing alternating current via electromagnetic induction.

Condenser & Vacuum Efficiency

Exhaust steam enters the condenser under vacuum conditions, increasing thermodynamic efficiency. Cooling water removes latent heat and condenses steam back to liquid form, allowing closed-loop secondary circulation.

Cooling Tower & Heat Rejection

A third independent cooling loop transfers waste heat to the cooling tower. Heat is dissipated through evaporative cooling. The visible plume consists of condensed water vapor, not smoke.
